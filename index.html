Our mission is to be at the heart of the village, providing services for the benefit of the community in a friendly, inclusive and supportive way

♥ Run entirely by volunteers

No-one is paid for any work. This includes those who work in the shop or behind the scenes. Everyone is there because they enjoy being part of the community

♥ wide range of products and services; locally sourced as much as possible

We use many of the excellent local producers. We stock speciality items; Hooting Owl gin and Lotte Shaws cakes for example.

♥ local specialities e.g. Crusties' bread and Starkey's pies and meat

Delicious freshly baked bread from Crusties of Sherburn. Pork pies, sausage rolls, quiches, speciality pies, cooked meat, sausages and bacon all from Starkeys.

♥ serving the village, surrounding areas and visitors

Many local people visit the shop frequently; many visitors working in the area also find the shop a great place to stock up on daily food and drink

♥ investing in the shop and village

Since the shop has no wages to pay, all profit is invested to improve the shop facilities, services or for initiates to support the local community

♥ place where people meet

Volunteers and customers meet by chance; others plan a meeting with coffee and cake in the garden or patio. Everyone is encouraged to take time to enjoy the facilities. Over winter the conservatory is offered as a warm space away from home.

The shop received the Queen's Award for Volunteering in 2022 - the highest level of recognition for voluntary organisations. Jo Ropner, the Lord Lieutenant of Yorkshire, presented the glass crystal, now proudly displayed in the shop, at a ceremony accompanied by a special cake. Around 60 volunteers toasted the honour and then enjoyed a takeaway supper.
